A Man with Golden Arm

James Christopher Harrison (born 27 December 1936), OAM, also known as the Man with the golden arm, is a blood plasma donor from Australia whose unusual plasma composition has been used to make a treatment for Rhesus disease. He has made over 1000 donations throughout his lifetime, and these donations are estimated to have saved over two million unborn babies from the condition.

Commenting on his record, he said:

"I could say it's the only record that I hope is broken, because if they do, they have donated a thousand donations."

Nominated for Australian of the Year
Medal of the Order of Australia (OAM) on 7 June 1999.
Nominated in the New South Wales Local Hero division of the Australian of the Year awards.
